Basket Weaving Techniques

Basket weaving techniques vary both in style and in skill level required to succeed. A refined basket weaver leaves no excess
wicker or wood sticking out of the basket that can catch, scrape or otherwise harm the basket's content or its user. On this page, we will
explore 3 different ways to weave baskets.

Below is a list of the weaving styles we will be covering.

  • Randing (easy)
  • Pairing (medium)
  • Waling (hard)

Randing

Randing is often considered the most simple type of basket weaving to do. As shown in the above image, it involves weaving
the wicker under one stake and then over the next, etc. The randing technique should be done with an odd number of stakes, lest
the weave become uneven.

Pairing

Pairing is where basket weaving becomes slightly more advanced. A more decorative approach than randing, pairing utilizes
2 weavers that twist around each other and are woven over the stakes. This is often used for rims of baskets where decoration is
a symbol of quality.

Waling

Waling is considered the most complicated, but strongest, method of basket weaving. As shown in the image above, it involves
weaving the wicker around the stakes in multiple different patterns, creating a potentially beautiful basket with exceptional strength.
